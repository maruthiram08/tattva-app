Your Inquiry

Why did Rama choose to exile Sita specifically to Valmiki’s ashram?

Rama did not explicitly strategically choose Valmiki’s ashram. The texts describe that Lakshmana, under Rama’s orders, took Sita to the banks of the Ganges near the Tamasa river. It was a location coincidentally close to the hermitage.

Upon being abandoned, Sita was found weeping by the sage’s disciples. Recognizing her distress and divine nature, Valmiki offered her sanctuary, citing his knowledge of her purity and his past friendship with King Dasharatha.

Source Text Uttara Kanda 49.10–18
"The sage Valmiki, hearing the sound of weeping, approached the grief-stricken Vaidehi and offered her protection within his hermitage."
Critical Edition, Baroda

Narrative Role

Valmiki’s intervention bridges the event and the epic's composition, as he later teaches the poem to Lava and Kusha there.

Interpretive Note

Unlike later retellings, the Valmiki Ramayana presents the abandonment as a severing of ties, with Valmiki’s aid being an independent act.
